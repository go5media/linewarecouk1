Dedicated Business Numbers - What's Even The Point? 

so!

You may be thinking "The landline is dead", and to a point you would be true. The house phone is not what it used to be, but here is the thing that most people don't realise when starting a small business... using a mobile for personal use, is very different to what a business requires, and having a dedicated number actually has strong advantages over just using a mobile number

A business number can, ironically, be more mobile than a mobile. Here's how.

Now, "If I get a business number, what will happen when I'm not in the office?" You may believe that means either "I have to pay someone to sit and answer the phone" or "I will miss work." Originally, that was the case, but these days your business number is no longer tied to a jack on the wall. Nowadays, clients can reach you wherever you are, in your office via a traditional desk phone, on the go via your laptop, and up a ladder via a mobile app when using the LW Plus Addon.

But a business number that only works when someone calls you is only doing half the job. It's not just receiving calls, it's making them too. Presenting your business number to clients and suppliers matters, as it reduces the "I didn't recognise the number" or "I don't answer calls from withheld numbers" problem that every tradesperson has run into at least once.

Wait! so? I don't need to turn off my device to stop getting business calls, tell me more.

With the click of a button you will stop reciveing inbound business calls to all devices on that user, there's even an option to only stop inbound from call queues only so that only the people you want calling you can reach you, but what if I need to calls a client or supplier? Not worry, when you turn on do not distube or log out of call queue, It's doesn't dissable your ablity to make outbound telephones, it only effefts inbound calls

Can I personal, professional and local

Yes, using VoIP or Voice over IP allows you to use pretty much any number from anywhere in the world, even if you don't live there or are not currently in that country. You can get numbers from the UK, US, Ireland, and lots more places.

What you can get in the UK: 01, 02, 03, 07, 08, 09.

It is down to the provider you choose as to which numbers you can use. For example, the provider we use ourselves and for our clients allows us to select from 01, 02, 03, 07, and 08 numbers.
