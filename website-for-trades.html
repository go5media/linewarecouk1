Would A Website Even Help My Plastering Businesses?

By Line Ware | Mar 14 2026 12:32 | 2 Minute Read

Dedicated Website -
Why does it work? 

Right - but I have already got an Instagram

You have already got an Instagram. Your best jobs are on there, customers have found you through it, and it has done the job so far. The question is a fair one, what does a website actually add to that?

The honest answer is that Instagram and a website are not doing the same thing, even when they are showing the same work. Instagram is borrowed space. You are building something on a platform that owns the land, sets the rules, and can change both without asking you first. Your reach on any given post is decided by an algorithm you have no control over. Your page looks the way Instagram has decided pages look. And if someone wants to pass your details on to a friend who is not on the platform, the whole thing hits a wall.

A website is yours in a way that Instagram simply is not. It behaves how you build it, it looks how you want it to look, and it is there on your terms permanently. The two work better together than either one does alone, your Instagram brings people to the door, and your website is what they walk into.

Your Work, Your Gallery, Your Way

Plastering is one of the trades where the finished result genuinely sells itself. A clean skim, a venetian finish, a seamless repair, these things photograph well and they do the convincing before anyone has picked up the phone. Instagram shows that work on the platform's schedule, in the platform's format, surrounded by everything else competing for attention at the same time.
A gallery on your own website is a different experience entirely. Your best jobs are front and centre every time someone lands on the page. You decide the order, the grouping, the context. There are no adverts either side of it, no algorithm deciding whether today is a good day to show it, and no other content pulling focus away from what you are trying to show. The customer is looking at your work, in your space, with nothing else in the room.

More Than One Way To Get In Touch

Not everyone contacts a business the same way. Some people call. Some people fill in a form because they are in a meeting and cannot make a phone call right now. Some people want to send a quick message and wait for a reply. If the only route to you is a direct message on Instagram, you are putting a gate in front of everyone who prefers to do it differently.
A website removes that gate. Phone number, contact form, whichever channels suit your business, all in one place, all available at the same time. The customer uses whichever one works for them, and you do not lose the enquiry because the method did not match.
